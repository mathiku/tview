What every column means

The scan looks for healthy uptrends that have dipped back to the 100-day moving average — a spot where a rising stock often finds support and bounces. A moving average (SMA) is just the average closing price over the last N bars; the 100-day line smooths out the noise and tends to act like a floor in an uptrend.

★ Pinned stocks always appear in the overview (manage on the Watchlist page). Random large caps fill out the rest of today's scan (~55/day from a ~170-stock pool).

The SMA columns (D / W / M · 100 / 200)

How far price sits above (green, +%) or below (red, −%) a moving average, on Daily, Weekly and Monthly bars. Above the line = uptrend on that timeframe. SMAs+ counts how many of the six lines price is above (6/6 = strongest).

Watchlist checks

A stock earns a watchlist entry only when all three are ✓ — the trend is intact, it has pulled back to the 100 SMA, and it was recently higher (so this is a genuine dip, not a stock stuck flat on the line).

  • ✓ Trend OK — weekly & monthly above the 200 SMA, or 5+/6 SMAs bullish (the bigger-picture uptrend is healthy)
  • ✓ At 100 — daily price is within ±3% of the 100 SMA (it has come back to support)
  • ✓ Was high — was ≥5% above the daily 100 SMA within the last ~4 weeks (it dropped into support rather than sitting on it)

Pattern confirms

Extra clues from the recent daily candles that the dip may be ending and buyers are stepping in. More ✓ = higher rank within the watchlist — none of these are required.

  • ✓ Hammer — a candle with a long lower wick and small body: price sold off during the day but closed back near the top, rejecting the lows
  • ✓ 3↑↓ — three up days followed by 1–2 down days: a short, orderly pullback into the moving average rather than a collapse
  • ✓ Vol — volume confirms: the down days traded lighter than the prior up-leg (weak selling), or a hammer printed on above-average volume (strong buying)
  • ✓ 2B — double bottom, the "W" shape: price bottomed, bounced, dropped to about the same low a second time, and is now climbing back up through the middle peak (the "neckline"). Two failed attempts to push lower is a classic sign the downtrend is exhausting. A cyan ✓ means it's on the last leg right now (just reclaiming the neckline) — the only actionable case. A yellow ● means a double bottom broke out earlier and has moved on; hover to see how many bars ago.

Suggested levels (Stop · Target · Gain)

A rough trade plan if you bought near the current price — not advice, just the arithmetic.

  • Stop — where you'd cut the loss, placed just below the recent swing low. If price falls here, the setup has failed.
  • Target — a profit goal set at 1.2× the distance to the stop (so the potential reward is bigger than the risk).
  • Gain — the % you'd make from the current price if the target is hit.

The Signal column

A one-glance verdict for each stock, best setups first. The suffix after a "·" names whichever pattern confirmed (e.g. Pullback · hammer).

  • Pullback Passed all three watchlist checks — a real dip to the 100 SMA in a healthy uptrend. The setup we're hunting for.
  • Near 100 At the 100 SMA in a good trend, but it never stretched high first — sitting on support rather than dipping into it.
  • Trend OK Uptrend is intact and it was recently extended, but price isn't back at the 100 SMA yet. One to watch.
  • Extended Trend is mostly bullish but price is still well above the 100 SMA — no pullback to buy.
  • Mixed Some moving averages bullish, some not — no clear trend.
  • Weak trend Mostly below its moving averages. Not a candidate.
